Brainstorm Sessions

Affiliated event Eurocrypt 2023

Saturday, 22 April, Lyon, France



Summary

The Brainstorm Session consists of two parts: In the morning, there will be a series of ``idea pitches'' (that have been prepared beforehand by certain participants, see \cref{sec:sub}) consisting of a presentation of about 15-20 minutes on a specific topic in isogeny-based cryptography, leading to a potential research question in the afternoon, we focus in groups of 4 to 6 participants on working on (a selection of) these potential research questions, with the hope of leading to actual progress. If there are too many proposed ideas in the morning for the number of working groups, we hold a vote to select the ideas with the most potential to work on.



Submission

Leading up to this event, we will promote it through the Isogeny Club, and put out a ``call for ideas'', where anyone can suggest a research idea to work on at the Brainstorm Day. There will be no selection process for the submissions, as we assume we will have enough time in the morning to showcase all properly worked-out ideas that we receive. If the number of properly worked-out ideas exceeds the capacity of the working groups in the afternoon, we hold a vote by the participants to decide what to work on in these groups.
