<p>An adaptor signature extends an existing digital signature scheme by adding the ability to generate pre-signatures, signature-like objects which can later be converted into true signatures by revealing an ephemeral secret. Adaptor signatures can replace on-chain scripts for blockchain applications such as atomic swap—ensuring fair play when two users wish to exchange goods. Unfortunately, most known adaptor signature constructions are vulnerable to quantum attack; the only known post-quantum constructions are one from lattices and two from isogenies. Of these two, the first (Tairi et al., FC 2021) has pre-signatures several times larger than those of the underlying signature scheme CSI-FiSh, while the second (Jana et al., CANS 2024) requires modification to the underlying scheme. We present a construction that attains optimal pre-signature sizes while remaining compatible with standard CSI-FiSh.</p>
